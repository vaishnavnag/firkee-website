01 How It Works

Watching every stage

01

Cut

Pattern alignment is verified against a digital master. Any deviation beyond 0.5mm triggers an immediate flag before the leather moves to the next bench.

Tolerance: ±0.5mm
02

Lasting

Sole alignment and heel positioning are checked against spec. Asymmetry and adhesion gaps are flagged in real time by visual sensors on the last bench.

Alignment: ±1mm
03

Stitching

Stitch tension, stitch count per inch, and thread colour consistency are monitored continuously. Variance beyond spec triggers a stop-and-check alert.

Most common flag point
04

Quality Control

AQL 2.5 inspection, automated. Colour deviation, sole separation, embellishment check, and finish consistency — all scored and logged before a pair advances to packing.

AQL 2.5 standard
05

Packing

Batch completion is verified against the order spec. Size assortment, box labelling, and carton count are all cross-checked before a batch is marked dispatch-ready.

Batch verified before dispatch

03 What Data Catches

Common defects, stopped early

Stitching

Tension Variance

Loose or tight stitching that changes under wear. Data monitoring detects thread tension deviation per stitch cycle.

72% of stitch-stage flags
Lasting

Sole Gap

Adhesion failure between upper and sole. Detected by gap sensor at the lasting press before bonding sets.

54% of lasting-stage flags
QC

Colour Deviation

Dye lot inconsistency between pairs in the same batch. Colour delta is compared to the approved lab dip.

48% of QC-stage flags
Cut

Pattern Misalignment

Grain or texture misalignment across paired components. Flagged before the leather is cut, saving material.

38% of cut-stage flags
Stitching

Skipped Stitch

Missed stitch cycles that create structural weak points. Vision sensor counts stitches per inch in real time.

29% of stitch-stage flags
Packing

Size Mismatch

Wrong size in a carton. Barcode scan at packing verified against the order pick list before the box seals.

18% of pack-stage flags
